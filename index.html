onfessions for a city that forgets its soul.

A small, haunted press devoted to moral collisions, late-night prayers, and the oppressive warmth of a single lamp. Here we publish fragments for those who suspect that conscience is a kind of fever.

Manifesto

On guilt as architecture

We build with guilt the way others build with stone. Each column is a confession, each arch a reluctant forgiveness. The city stands not on reason, but on fevered mercy.

On the doubled self

A person is never singular. We publish the notes of those who hear their other selves whispering in corridors and on bridges, insisting that the heart is never finished speaking.

On the underground vow

To remain tender is a form of rebellion. We keep a watch for those small acts of mercy that refuse the tyranny of the obvious.

Editor's Margins

I am not even a spiteful man. I am not an embittered man. I am convinced my liver is diseased. Still, I insist on writing.
