Notes

Marginalia from a conscience at war.

Note I: The mirror and the judge

To see oneself clearly is to be condemned by a gentler tribunal. We advise our readers to keep a mirror, but not to stare too long.

Note II: A fever of mercy

Mercy is rarely clean. It arrives with mud on its boots and a trembling hand. We count on that tremor as proof of its authenticity.

Note III: The crowded room

In every room sits a crowd of unseen selves, each with their own confession. The only peace is to acknowledge them and offer a chair.
